# AZ511, Traffic England, and Public Road Data Continuity

## Why road data belongs in this project

Road information is not just convenience data. It affects emergency response, public safety, commuting, logistics, journalism, civic oversight, and resident awareness. When road information becomes less public and more dependent on private providers, residents lose the ability to independently inspect how public infrastructure is working.

Overture tracks road data because ALPR debates and public camera debates sit inside a wider infrastructure question: who controls visibility into public roads?

## Arizona model: AZ511

AZ511 is Arizona's public traveler-information layer. It is the first place this project points people for Arizona road conditions, incidents, alerts, camera views when available, construction, and travel conditions.

Primary links:

- AZ511: https://www.az511.gov/
- ADOT: https://azdot.gov/

## Traffic England closure context

The public closure notice supplied to FLLC says Traffic England will close on 30 June 2026 and directs users to navigation apps, National Highways, downloadable road-closure data, live incidents, and NTIS Transformed Solution access for industry professionals.

This is important for U.S. policy because it shows a pattern:

1. A public-facing traffic portal becomes unavailable or reduced.
2. The public is redirected to large navigation providers or official pages with narrower surfaces.
3. Industry users may still access raw/professional feeds.
4. Residents lose the old direct camera or traffic interface.

That does not mean every private navigation source is bad. It means public-road information should have a durable public layer that does not depend entirely on private intermediaries.

## Public data principle

For public roads, the public should know:

- what data exists
- who collects it
- who stores it
- what is public
- what is private
- what is archived
- what is sold or licensed
- what is shared with third parties
- how errors are corrected
- how decisions are audited

## Difference between traffic cameras and ALPR

Traffic camera:

- usually monitors road conditions, congestion, crashes, weather, and traffic flow
- may show live or still imagery to the public
- may be operated by a transportation agency
- should still have retention, access, and privacy rules

ALPR camera:

- scans license plates
- creates location/time records
- can compare against hotlists
- can be queried later
- can become part of a multi-jurisdictional movement database
- requires stronger governance because the privacy risk is higher

## Arizona policy demand

Arizona should maintain a public-road information layer while restricting mass location tracking.

Suggested policy language:

> Public road data generated by public agencies should remain publicly documented and accessible when lawful and safe. ALPR-derived vehicle-location data should be treated separately from traffic-condition data and should require stronger controls, retention limits, access logs, warrant rules, and public reporting.

## FLLC stance

FLLC supports useful traffic safety information and public road awareness. FLLC opposes opaque private mass-tracking systems that create searchable movement histories without strong public controls.
