# Public Records and Public Comment Toolkit

This toolkit is for lawful civic action. It is designed to create a public record without harassment, spam, impersonation, or interference.

## Public-records request template

Subject: Public records request: ALPR / Flock / public safety camera contracts, policies, and audit logs

Hello,

I am requesting public records related to automated license plate readers, Flock Safety, public safety cameras, traffic cameras, or vendor-operated camera systems used by [CITY / AGENCY / CAMPUS / HOA / DEPARTMENT].

Please provide records covering:

1. Current and past contracts, purchase orders, invoices, renewals, amendments, and statements of work.
2. Camera locations, camera IDs, installation dates, and device types.
3. Data retention settings and retention-policy documents.
4. Search policies, access policies, and user-role lists.
5. Audit logs showing searches, hits, external sharing, and administrative access.
6. Hotlist sources and procedures for adding or removing hotlists.
7. Data-sharing agreements with outside agencies, regional networks, federal agencies, vendors, or private entities.
8. Warrant, subpoena, court-order, or emergency-request procedures.
9. Misuse investigations, complaints, policy violations, and disciplinary records related to camera or ALPR use.
10. Vendor breach notices, security assessments, penetration-test summaries, cyber incident notices, or access-control reviews.
11. Public meeting agendas, staff reports, privacy-impact assessments, or legal memos related to approval or renewal.
12. Any documents explaining whether data may be sold, licensed, disclosed, exported, or used for analytics beyond the original public-safety purpose.

If any portion is withheld, please cite the specific legal exemption and release all segregable non-exempt material.

Please provide records electronically.

Thank you,

[NAME]
[CONTACT]

## Public-comment script

My name is [NAME]. I am asking this body not to approve or renew any ALPR, Flock, or public-safety camera contract without a public accountability framework.

This system may affect ordinary residents who are not suspected of anything. Vehicle-location data can reveal work, worship, medical visits, family routines, legal visits, political activity, and personal associations. Public safety does not require secret data retention or silent sharing.

Before approval, please publish:

- the full contract
- camera locations
- retention settings
- audit-log policy
- sharing partners
- search rules
- warrant policy
- misuse penalties
- annual performance metrics
- renewal date

I am asking for a pause, a public registry, a maximum retention limit, warrant-based external sharing, and an annual public vote.

Public safety should be public, accountable, and limited.

## Questions for city council, campus leadership, HOA boards, and police departments

1. What exact crime or safety problem justifies the system?
2. What less invasive alternatives were considered?
3. How many cameras are installed or planned?
4. Are the locations public?
5. Who owns the cameras?
6. Who owns the data?
7. Who can search the data?
8. Is every search logged?
9. How long is data retained?
10. Are outside agencies allowed to search or request data?
11. Is a warrant required for external disclosure?
12. Can the vendor disclose data without city permission?
13. Can the vendor sell, license, train on, or analyze the data for other purposes?
14. What happens after a false hit?
15. How many false hits have occurred?
16. How many crimes were solved?
17. How many non-criminal searches occurred?
18. How are misuse complaints handled?
19. What security controls protect the database?
20. When does the contract expire?

## Contract redlines to demand

- No sale of resident data.
- No data use outside the stated public-safety purpose.
- Public camera registry.
- Maximum retention limit.
- Role-based access control.
- Search reason and case number required.
- Audit logs available for inspection.
- External disclosure only with warrant or court order.
- City notice before vendor disclosure.
- Breach notice within a defined time.
- Annual independent audit.
- Misuse penalties.
- Annual public renewal vote.
- Sunset clause.

## Campaign discipline

Use accurate claims. Link sources. Ask hard questions. Keep records. Publish summaries.

Do not:

- spam officials
- mail-bomb anyone
- use phishing tools
- impersonate agencies
- harass staff
- damage equipment
- bypass or access camera systems
- publish private personal information

A clean paper trail beats chaos.
